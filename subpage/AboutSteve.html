Steve's life and achievements


He was largely responsible for helping revive Apple, which had been on the verge of bankruptcy. He worked closely with designer Jony Ive to develop a line of products that had larger cultura ramifications, beginning in 1997 with the "Think different" advertising campaign and leading to the iMac, iTunes, iTunes Store, Apple Store, iPod, iPhone, App Store, and the iPad. In 2001, the original Mac OS was replaced with a completely new Mac OS X, based on NeXT's NeXTSTEP platform, giving the OS a modern Unix-based foundation for the first time. Jobs was diagnosed with a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor in 2003. He died of respiratory arrest related to the tumor at age 56 on October 5, 2011


Steve's Top10 Achievements

  1. Igniting the personal computer revolution.
  2. Introducing the mouse and graphical user interface.
  3. Bringing portable music players to the masses.
  4. Revolutionizing the sale of music.
  5. Shepherding the age of computer animation.
  6. Opening Apple stores.
  7. Making personal computers stylish.
  8. Launching the smartphone revolution.
  9. Changing how software applications are distributed.
  10. Kicking off the tablet trend.
